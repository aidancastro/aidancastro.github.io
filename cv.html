Research Experience

Current

Undergraduate Research Assistant — mmWave SFCW MIMO-SAR Imaging

USF Electrical Engineering · Advisor: Dr. Stavros Vakalis · Tampa, FL

Developing a mmWave SFCW MIMO-SAR imaging testbed using the IMAGEVK-74 module (20 Tx, 20 Rx) mounted on a 3D-printer stage; implementing coherent 3D backprojection in MATLAB for virtual-aperture reconstruction across a 15×15 virtual array, validated using OptiTrack. Investigating calibration techniques for bistatic MIMO imaging from 62–69 GHz.

Current

Undergraduate Research Assistant — RF Fingerprinting & Chaotic Antenna Array Authentication

USF Electrical Engineering · Advisor: Dr. Yasin Yilmaz · Tampa, FL

Chaotic antenna array (CAA) systems intentionally randomize antenna element positions and feed-line lengths to generate direction-dependent phase signatures (RF fingerprints) orders of magnitude stronger than naturally occurring IC hardware imperfections under realistic Rician fading channels, using deep-learning classifiers (ResNet-50, Xception, VGG-16, InceptionV3) trained on received I/Q samples.

Optimizing the ESP32 firmware that drives the array's RF switching network — restructuring the element-switching routine to minimize reconfiguration latency and timing jitter between antenna states, yielding faster and more repeatable per-configuration RF-fingerprint capture.

Project Experience

EEL 4461

Dual-Band PIFA Design — LTE Band 3 & 8

Antenna Theory · USF

Designed a compact dual-band PIFA with branch-line slit, L-shaped shorting wall, and slotted ground plane within a 35×10×5.7 mm footprint on RO4350B substrate (εr = 3.66), targeting LTE Band 8 (900 MHz) and LTE Band 3 (1800 MHz) to maximize bandwidth recovery.

EEL 4422

1.9 GHz LNA Design — Infineon BFP420

RF/Microwave Circuits II · USF

Designed a 1.9 GHz LNA using the Infineon BFP420 BJT: DC bias network, simultaneous noise and power match, and unconditional stability verification via Rollett K-factor and stability circles. Layout-level cosimulation with LineCalc-based microstrip element sizing; input/output matching networks designed using ideal and parasitic Modelithics components.

EEL 4422

900 MHz LNA Design — NXP PBR941

RF/Microwave Circuits II · USF

Voltage-feedback bias topology with DC feed/block sizing; stability analysis (Rollett K-factor, μ-factor, stability circles) and gain / noise-figure optimization in ADS.

EEL 4461

Rectangular Microstrip Patch Antenna

Antenna Theory · USF

Calculated patch dimensions (length, width, feed position) analytically in MATLAB via the transmission-line model; implemented a coaxial probe feed in HFSS with lumped port assignment and validated the S11 response.
